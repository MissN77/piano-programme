Sight Reading 👁️

Sight reading means playing a piece of music you've never seen before. It's a key skill for the ABRSM Grade 1 exam!

🎯 Today you'll learn:
✅ What sight reading is
✅ The 3-step method: SCAN → SPOT → PLAY
✅ How to keep going even if you make a mistake
✅ Practise on a short unseen melody
💡 In the ABRSM exam you get 30 seconds to look at the piece before playing. Use it wisely!

The 3-Step Method 🔍

Before you play a single note, spend your 30 seconds doing this:

1
SCAN the piece
Look at the time signature (3/4? 4/4?), the key signature (any sharps or flats?), and how long the piece is.
2
SPOT the tricky bits
Find any jumps, dotted rhythms, or unfamiliar patterns. Tap the rhythm on your knee first.
3
PLAY and keep going
Once you start, never stop. If you miss a note, skip it and keep the beat going. The examiner rewards continuity!
💡 ASD tip: It's OK to make mistakes — the rule is: KEEP. GOING. Even professionals play wrong notes; they just carry on!

Practice Tips 💡

🎯 This week, practise:
1. Use the 3-step method every time you see new music
2. Tap rhythms on your knee before playing
3. Try playing familiar songs with one hand only — to build note-reading speed
4. Look ahead — your eyes should always be one beat ahead of your hands!
💡 ASD tip:
Sight reading gets easier with repetition. The more NEW pieces you try, the faster your brain learns to decode notes. It's like reading — you got better at words by reading lots of them!
