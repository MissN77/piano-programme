Welcome to Lesson 10!

Today we learn Frère Jacques — a French round that everyone knows. We'll also introduce your Left Hand for the first time!

🎯 Today's GoalsPlay Frère Jacques with your Right Hand. Add a simple Left Hand note (C3) on beat 1 of each bar.

Notes used today: C D E F G

All from the C Major Scale you learned last lesson!

🎹 Notes on Your Keyboard

Click each highlighted key to hear it:

Right Hand FingeringC=1 · D=2 · E=3 · F=4 · G=5
Use fingers 1–5, one note per finger. No thumb-under needed here!
🤚 Left Hand Note
Play C3 (one octave below Middle C) with your Left Hand finger 5 (little finger) on beat 1 of each bar. Just one note — keep it simple!

Practise Hands Separate First

  1. Play RH melody through slowly
  2. Play LH alone — just one C at the start of each group of 4 beats
  3. When both feel secure, try putting them together
💡 Coordination tipStart by tapping the rhythm on your knees — RH taps the melody rhythm, LH taps once per bar. Then transfer to the keyboard.

📖 Left Hand — Bass Clef

The Left Hand reads from the Bass Clef (𝄢). This sits below the Treble Clef in the Grand Staff.

💡 Bass Clef NotesThe two dots sit around the F line — so Bass Clef is sometimes called the F Clef. Notes for the LH: G B D F A (Good Boys Do Fine Always).
📋 What to remember

For now, just remember: LH plays C3 — two white keys to the left of Middle C, found with your left little finger.

⭐ This Week's Practice

Daily Plan (10–15 mins)

Days 1–2: C Major Scale warm-up, then Frère Jacques RH only.

Days 3–4: Add LH C on beat 1 of each bar. HS then HT.

Days 5–6: Full piece HT at ♩=60. Can you sing and play?

Day 7: Performance for a family member!
