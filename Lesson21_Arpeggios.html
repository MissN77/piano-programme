You've learned chords — notes played together. An arpeggio is when you play those same notes one at a time, flowing upward like a wave!

Theory: Broken Chords 📚

What is an Arpeggio?

🎵 C major arpeggio:
C – E – G – C (going up)
C – G – E – C (going down)

These are the same notes as the C major chord — just played separately!

Why do Arpeggios Sound Beautiful?

Because the notes are all part of the same chord family, they naturally sound good together. The flowing motion creates a smooth, rippling sound.

💡 ABRSM Grade 1: You'll need to play broken chord patterns in your exam. Arpeggios are a key technique that examiners look for!
💡 Pattern tip: C–E–G is always the C major chord. You can start arpeggios from any note in the chord — just keep them in order!

Practice Tips 💡

🎯 This week, practise:
1. Play C–E–G as a block chord, then as an arpeggio
2. Try the arpeggio slowly, one note at a time
3. Listen for the flowing, harp-like sound
4. Try going up AND down the arpeggio
💡 ASD tip:
Arpeggios have a very predictable, regular pattern — up and down. Once you learn the pattern, it repeats and feels very satisfying to play!
