🎯 Today you'll do:

💡 In the exam you'll do all of this in one go — usually around 15 minutes. Today is your practice exam. Go for it!

ABRSM Grade 1 Format 📋

Here's exactly what happens in the exam — in order:

1. Pieces (approx. 9 minutes)
• List A piece — lively, crisp, energetic
• List B piece — lyrical, expressive, singing tone
• List C piece — characterful, fun, with personality
• You can play them in any order you prefer
2. Scales & Arpeggios (approx. 2 minutes)
• C major — one octave, hands separately
• G major — one octave, hands separately
• A minor — one octave, hands separately
3. Sight Reading (approx. 1 minute)
• A short 4-bar passage you haven't seen before
• You get 30 seconds to look at it first
4. Aural Tests (approx. 3 minutes)
• Clap the pulse • Identify changes in tempo or dynamics
• Sing back a short phrase
💡 ASD tip: Knowing the exact order removes surprises. Run through this list in your head before you go in. The exam is very predictable — that's helpful!

Practice Tips 💡

🎯 This week, practise:
1. Do another full run-through at least twice before the exam
2. Time yourself — the whole thing should take about 15 minutes
3. If a piece goes wrong, keep going — don't stop and restart
4. Practise walking in, sitting down, and beginning calmly — it helps!
💡 ASD tip:
A full run-through can feel overwhelming. Break it into two halves if needed: pieces first, scales and aural second. Or do it in a familiar room, in your usual practice spot — that reduces anxiety about the format.
