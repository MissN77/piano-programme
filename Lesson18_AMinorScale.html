Theory: Minor Scales 📚

Major vs Minor

Major scales sound happy and bright. Minor scales sound sad or mysterious. The difference is in which notes are raised or lowered.

🎵 A harmonic minor scale:
A – B – C – D – E – F – G# – A

The G# (sharp) is the "leading note" — it wants to resolve up to A.

Why "Harmonic" Minor?

There are a few types of minor scale. The harmonic minor has the raised 7th (G#) which creates a strong pull back to the home note A. This is the one used in ABRSM Grade 1.

💡 Memory trick: Count the gaps — minor scales have a smaller (minor) third step. A to C is only 3 semitones (not 4 like in C major).
