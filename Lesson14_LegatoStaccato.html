📖 Legato vs Staccato

Music can be played in very different ways. Today's two are the most common!

〰️
Legato

Smooth and connected. Each note flows into the next. Written with a curved line (slur).

🐌 "Silky and smooth"

•
Staccato

Short and bouncy. Lift your finger quickly after each note. Written with a dot above/below.

🐇 "Short and springy"

💡 Try pressing a key slowly and holding it (legato), then tap it very quickly and lift off fast (staccato). Notice the difference!
🧡 You might prefer one style over the other — both are completely fine to practise!

🎵 Can-Can (Offenbach)

This famous tune is all about staccato — play it short and lively! The Can-Can should feel like dancing feet.

🐢 🐇 90 BPM
🧡 This tune goes fast! Start at a slow tempo and speed up only when it feels easy.

✅ Practice Tips

💡 Legato exercise: Play C–D–E–F–G slowly, keeping each key held until the next one sounds. Smooth!
💡 Staccato exercise: Play the same C–D–E–F–G but lift each finger quickly. Bouncy!
🧡 If Can-Can feels too fast, it's totally fine to play it at slow tempo — that still counts as great practice!
