Lesson 3 – Ode to Joy (Simplified)

Phase 1 · Week 3 · Five-Finger Position: C D E F G

Week 3

Welcome to Lesson 3! 🎶

You already know C, D, E — this week we add F and G to make your full five-finger position. Then we play Beethoven's famous Ode to Joy!

🎯 What You'll Learn

  • 🖐️ The complete five-finger position: C–D–E–F–G (fingers 1–2–3–4–5)
  • 🎵 Play Ode to Joy (simplified first phrase)
  • 📍 Find F and G on the keyboard
💡 Tip: Five fingers, five notes — one finger for each note. We'll build up slowly!

🎹 Five-Finger Position: C to G

Place your right hand so each finger rests on one note:

New Notes This Week:
F = just to the right of E (the note before the group of 3 black keys)
G = one step above F
💡 Tip: Don't move your hand — all five notes can be played without moving your thumb or pinky. That's the magic of five-finger position!

🖐️ Five-Finger Warm-Up

Play each finger in order, up then down: C–D–E–F–G–F–E–D–C

Fingers: 1–2–3–4–5–4–3–2–1

  • Place all five fingers before you start
  • Press each key gently — don't press hard!
  • Keep your wrist relaxed (not stiff)
  • Try 3 times — up and back each time
💡 Tip: The warm-up is a bit like a ladder — go up the steps, then come back down!

🎵 Ode to Joy – First Phrase

Composed by Beethoven. This is the most famous melody ever written! We play the first phrase today.

Notes: E–E–F–G–G–F–E–D–C–C–D–E–E–D–D
