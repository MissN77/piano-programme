About List B 📋

List B pieces have a singing, expressive character. They often move more slowly and ask for beautiful tone rather than crisp articulation.

🎵 List B Style Features:
• Smooth, connected legato throughout
• Expressive dynamics — shape each phrase like a song
• Often slower tempo than List A
• Romantic or 20th-century character
🎼 What examiners look for:
• Tone quality — does it sound beautiful?
• Phrasing — does the melody breathe?
• Sensitivity — soft where it needs to be soft
• Continuity — keep flowing even through mistakes
💡 ASD tip: Try imagining the melody as a human voice. Would a singer rush through it? Probably not — so take your time and let each note ring.

Practice Tips 💡

🎯 This week, practise:
1. Play the melody very slowly — focus on beautiful tone
2. Add a slight swell (get louder then softer) within each phrase
3. Listen to real Grade 1 List B pieces on YouTube
4. Ask yourself: "Does it sound like someone singing?"
💡 ASD tip:
If the piece feels too emotional or expressive to connect with, try assigning it a clear image: a bird flying, a boat on a calm lake, a slow sunrise. Your chosen image will guide your playing naturally.
