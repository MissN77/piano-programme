Theory: D Natural Minor 📚

The D Minor Scale

🎵 D natural minor scale:
D – E – F – G – A – Bb – C – D

Notice: F is natural (not sharp), and B is flat. These are the key differences from D major.

Relative Keys

D minor is the relative minor of F major — they share the same key signature (one flat: Bb). Just starting the same notes from a different note creates a totally different mood!

💡 Memory trick: D minor starts on D. Count up 3 semitones from D and you get F — that's its relative major. Every minor key has a relative major 3 semitones higher!
