Exam Pieces: List A 🎼

Welcome to Phase 4 — Exam Prep! The ABRSM Grade 1 exam has three lists of pieces (A, B and C). List A pieces are usually lively and energetic — Baroque or Classical style.

🎯 Today you'll learn:
✅ What the ABRSM exam lists mean
✅ How List A pieces sound and feel
✅ Key performance skills: tempo, articulation, dynamics
✅ Play a List A style melody
💡 In the exam you play one piece from each list — you and your teacher choose which ones to prepare. Today we explore the List A style!

About List A 📋

List A pieces have a bright, crisp character. Think marches, dances, and lively tunes from the Baroque and Classical periods.

🎵 List A Style Features:
• Clear, steady pulse — keep the beat going!
• Crisp articulation — some notes short, some connected
• Not too loud, not too quiet — confident and clear
• Hand position matters — sit up straight!
🎼 What examiners look for:
• Correct notes and rhythms
• Appropriate tempo (not too fast!)
• Musical character — does it sound like a List A piece?
• Continuity — keep going through mistakes
💡 ASD tip: It helps to have a clear picture of the piece. Is it a march? A dance? A sunrise? Decide before you play and let that image guide your fingers.

Practice Tips 💡

🎯 This week, practise:
1. Play the melody at a slow, steady tempo first
2. Add dynamics — make bar 1 slightly louder, bar 3 slightly softer
3. Listen to real Grade 1 List A pieces on YouTube
4. Ask yourself: "Does it sound like a march?"
💡 ASD tip:
It's fine to practise the same section over and over. Pick your favourite 4 notes and play just those until they feel perfect. Then add more notes gradually.
