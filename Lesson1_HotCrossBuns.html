Step 2 of 5

🤸 Finger Warm-Up

Before we play the tune, let's wake up our fingers! These exercises help your fingers move independently — like stretching before sport.

🎯 Goal: Press each key firmly and clearly — one at a time. Listen to each note ring out before moving to the next.

Exercise 1 – Walk Up C to G

Place your right hand with thumb on C. Walk fingers 1→2→3→4→5 up to G. Then walk back 5→4→3→2→1.

Exercise 2 – Just G, A, B

Now move your hand so thumb is on G. Press G (1) → A (2) → B (3) slowly. Then B (3) → A (2) → G (1). Do this 4 times.

💡 Keep your wrist relaxed and level — not raised up or drooping. Imagine you're holding a small ball in your palm.

Exercise 3 – Rhythm Clapping

Before playing, clap and say the words aloud to feel the rhythm:

HOT – cross – BUNS – (rest)
HOT – cross – BUNS – (rest)
one – a – PEN – ny,
two – a – PEN – ny,
HOT – cross – BUNS – (rest)

Bold = strong beat. Tap your knee on the strong beats!

Step 3 of 5

🍞 Why Hot Cross Buns?

Hot Cross Buns is the perfect first piece because:

  • It only uses 3 notes — B, A, G
  • The melody steps downwards — easy to remember
  • It teaches you to feel a steady beat
  • It sounds like a real tune straight away! 🎉
🎹 Hand position: Right hand, thumb (1) on G, index (2) on A, middle (3) on B.

🗝 How to Remember B, A, G

  • G — right after the group of three black keys. Say "three blacks, then G!"
  • A — one white key up from G. They're neighbours.
  • B — between the last black key of three and C. Say "B is before C!"
✅ Say out loud: "G — A — B, go up three!" Repeat it until it sticks.

📋 Today's Practice Checklist

Tap each item when done!

  • Say finger numbers 1, 2, 3 out loud while pressing G, A, B
  • Walk up C→G five times (Exercise 1)

  • Clap and say the Hot Cross Buns rhythm
  • Play Hot Cross Buns at ♩=60 three times
  • Play it at ♩=80 if ♩=60 felt easy
