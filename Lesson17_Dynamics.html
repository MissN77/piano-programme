Phase 3 · Week 17

Dynamics 🔊

Welcome to Phase 3! Today we learn about dynamics — how loud or soft to play.

🎯 Today you will:
✅ Learn piano (p) and forte (f)
✅ Understand 4 dynamic levels
✅ Play Für Elise opening with feeling

Find Your Notes 🔍

Today's notes: E · Eb · B · D · C · A
Watch out for Eb (D sharp/E flat)!

The black key between D and E is called D# or Eb.

💡 In Für Elise the melody alternates E and Eb very quickly — listen carefully!

Dynamic Markings 🎼

pp
Pianissimo
Very soft 🤫
mp
Mezzo piano
Moderately soft
mf
Mezzo forte
Moderately loud
ff
Fortissimo
Very loud! 📣
🔑 The key words:
Piano (p) = soft · Forte (f) = loud
Mezzo = medium/moderately
Hairpins 🎻
< = crescendo (getting louder)
> = decrescendo (getting softer)

Für Elise — Opening 🌙

One of Beethoven's most famous pieces. The opening alternates E and Eb!

Play softly (p) throughout — it's a gentle, dreamy piece.
Tap any note to hear it. Use Play to hear the melody.

Practice Tips 💡

🎯 This Week's Focus
Dynamics make music come alive. Play softly and feel the difference!
Step-by-step practice:
1️⃣ Play the E-Eb pattern slowly (it's tricky!)
2️⃣ Add the rest of the melody one bar at a time
3️⃣ Play the whole thing very softly (pp)
4️⃣ Try once at medium volume (mf) — notice the difference?
✅ You're ready to move on when:
• You can hear the difference between p and f when you play
• You can name all 4 dynamic markings
• The Für Elise opening sounds smooth and gentle
