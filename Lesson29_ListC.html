About List C 📋

List C pieces have a bold, characterful personality. Think folk songs, jazz-flavoured tunes, music from films and popular styles.

🎵 List C Style Features:
• Distinctive rhythm — often syncopated or bouncy
• Strong character — could be quirky, jazzy or playful
• Sometimes uses black notes or unusual scales
• Examiners want to see you enjoy playing it!
🎼 What examiners look for:
• Rhythmic energy — does it groove?
• Character — does it have personality?
• Confidence — commit to the piece!
• Continuity — keep going, no matter what
💡 ASD tip: List C pieces often have a clear "mood" — spooky, cheerful, mysterious. Pick one word that describes yours. That word is your guide for every single note you play.

Practice Tips 💡

🎯 This week, practise:
1. Play the melody with bags of personality — exaggerate the character
2. Clap the rhythm first before playing — feel the bounce
3. Listen to real Grade 1 List C pieces on YouTube
4. Ask yourself: "Does this piece have a character I can describe in one word?"
💡 ASD tip:
If expressing "character" feels vague, try this: play it as if you are acting. Are you a robot? A bouncy ball? A sneaky fox? Pick a character and let your fingers act it out.
