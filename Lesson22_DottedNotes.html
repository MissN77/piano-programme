Theory: The Dot Rule 📚

🎵 The dot rule:
A dot after a note adds half its value to it.

♩ Crotchet = 1 beat
♩. Dotted crotchet = 1 + ½ = 1½ beats
♪ Quaver = ½ beat

Dotted Crotchet + Quaver

These two notes together fill exactly 2 beats — one bar of 2/4 time, or half a bar of 4/4.

💡 Say it aloud: "LONG-short, LONG-short" while clapping. The LONG is 3× as long as the short — that's what gives dotted rhythms their distinctive bounce!
💡 Where you'll hear this:
Dotted rhythms appear in marches, jigs, and many Grade 1 exam pieces. Once you hear it, you'll notice it everywhere!

Practice Tips 💡

🎯 This week, practise:
1. Clap "LONG-short, LONG-short" before playing
2. Say the rhythm aloud as you play: "LONG-short"
3. Play slowly first, then speed up
4. Listen for dotted rhythms in music you hear!
💡 ASD tip:
Dotted rhythms can feel tricky at first because of the uneven feel. Clapping first then playing helps your body learn the pattern before your fingers have to do it.
