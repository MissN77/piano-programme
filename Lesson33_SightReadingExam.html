Sight Reading 👀

Sight reading is reading music you've never seen before and playing it straight away. It's one part of the ABRSM Grade 1 exam — and it's more manageable than it sounds.

🎯 Today you'll practise:
✅ The 30-second look-through strategy
✅ Spotting notes D E F G A before you play
✅ Reading a dotted rhythm
✅ Playing a short sight-reading melody
💡 In the exam you get 30 seconds to study the piece before you play. That time is gold — use it well!

The 30-Second Strategy 📋

Before you play a single note, use your 30 seconds to scan for these things:

1. Find the key
Look at the key signature. Any sharps or flats? That tells you which scale the piece is in — so you know which black keys to watch for.
2. Find the time signature
Is it 4/4? 3/4? That tells you how many beats per bar. Tap the pulse before you play.
3. Spot the highest and lowest notes
Look for any note that sits outside your comfort range — plan the fingering quickly.
4. Look for dotted rhythms
A dot adds half the note's value. A dotted crotchet = 1½ beats. A quaver = ½ beat. Together: 1½ + ½ = 2 beats.
Dotted rhythm feel: LOOOng–short, LOOOng–short
💡 ASD tip: Scan the piece like a checklist — key → time → range → rhythm. Doing the same steps every time makes sight reading feel less random and more predictable.
