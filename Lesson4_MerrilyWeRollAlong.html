🎵 Left Hand Time!

Today your left hand gets to be the star. We'll learn Merrily We Roll Along using just three fingers.

🎯 Today's Goals

By the end of this lesson you'll be able to:

  • Place your left hand in C position
  • Play a melody with your left hand
  • Recognise the bass clef (the left-hand clef)
  • Play C, D, E and G with your LH

📖 What's New Today

Left hand (LH) only — just like your right hand has a "home", so does your left hand. Today we put the LH pinky (finger 5) on C and work upward.

𝄢

That curly symbol is the bass clef. It shows left-hand notes. You'll meet it a lot!

💡 Sticker tip: Your left C and right C are the same note but played by different hands. Try a different colour sticker for your LH C to tell them apart.

🎵 The Bass Clef

On the bass clef staff, the lines from bottom to top spell: G – B – D – F – A

Remember: "Good Boys Do Fine Always"

The spaces from bottom to top spell: A – C – E – G

Remember: "All Cows Eat Grass"

For now, just know that C D E G sit in easy-to-find spots. Your stickers will help!

🔥 LH Warm-Up Exercises

Before the melody, wake up your left-hand fingers:

Exercise 1 – Walk Down and Up

Play slowly: E4 → D4 → C4, then back up: C4 → D4 → E4

Fingers: 3 → 4 → 5, then 5 → 4 → 3

3E
4D
5C

Exercise 2 – C to G Stretch

Play: C4 → G4 — pinky (5) to thumb (1). Keep your wrist relaxed!

💡 Relax check: Drop your arm from the shoulder, let it hang, shake your wrist gently. That's how relaxed your hand should feel while playing.

Exercise 3 – Count Along

Play E4–D4–C4 three times, counting aloud: "1–2–3, 1–2–3, 1–2–3"

Try to make each note the same length.

✅ Practice Checklist

Tap each item when you've done it:

  • Placed LH in C position (finger 5 on C)
  • Played E – D – C three times slowly
  • Played the full melody at ♩=60
  • Played the full melody at ♩=80
  • Played with eyes closed (listening only)

💡 Parent / Carer Notes

This week practise: 5–10 min daily. Focus on a relaxed LH position before playing.

  • If finger 5 curls too much, gently remind: "pretend you're holding a small ball"
  • The E–D–C shape (3–4–5) is the key pattern — drill this separately if needed
  • The jump to G (finger 1) can be tricky — practise just that jump by itself
  • Different-coloured sticker on LH C vs RH C really helps distinguish the two
🌟 ASD tip: Predictable routine helps. Always start practice with the same warm-up (E–D–C five times), then the song. Knowing "what comes next" reduces anxiety.
