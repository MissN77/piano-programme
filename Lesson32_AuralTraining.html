Aural Training 👂

The aural section of the ABRSM Grade 1 exam tests what your ears can do — not just your fingers. It's actually one of the most approachable parts once you know what to expect.

🎯 Today you'll practise:
✅ Clapping a steady pulse
✅ Identifying changes in tempo (faster / slower)
✅ Identifying changes in dynamics (louder / softer)
✅ Singing back a short two-note phrase
💡 The examiner plays the music — you listen and respond. No piano needed for this part!

What to Expect 📋

The ABRSM Grade 1 aural test takes about 3 minutes. Here's exactly what happens:

Test A — Pulse Clapping
The examiner plays a short piece. You clap along with the steady beat. That's it!
Key: find the regular pulse first, then clap with it — not every single note.
Test B — Changes in Character
The examiner plays a piece twice. You identify what changed — was it louder or softer? Faster or slower?
Key: listen to the overall feel, not individual notes.
Test C — Sing Back
The examiner plays a 2-note phrase. You sing it back on any vowel (like "la").
Key: you don't need to name the notes — just match the pitch.
💡 ASD tip: Knowing the exact structure means no surprises. These three tests are always in the same order and always very short.

Practice Tips 💡

🎯 This week, practise:
1. Clap along to any song you like — find the steady pulse
2. Listen to music and describe it: is it louder now? Faster?
3. Ask someone to hum two notes — try to sing them back
4. Practise on a YouTube Grade 1 aural test video
💡 ASD tip:
If singing out loud feels uncomfortable, try humming very quietly to yourself. In the exam, any vocal response counts — even a whisper. You can also ask to try again if you didn't hear the phrase clearly.
