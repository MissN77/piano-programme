Scales Drill 🎵

Scales are a key part of the ABRSM Grade 1 exam — and they're not just exercises. Practising scales builds the muscle memory and finger control that makes everything else easier.

🎯 Today you'll drill:
✅ C major — all white keys, one octave
✅ G major — one sharp (F#), one octave
✅ D major — two sharps (F#, C#), one octave
✅ A minor harmonic — raised 7th (G#), one octave
💡 In the exam, you play scales hands separately. Practise each hand on its own first — right hand, then left hand.

Scale Guide 📋

Every major scale has the same pattern of tones and semitones — but it starts on a different note. Here's what makes each scale special:

C major — C D E F G A B C
All white keys. No sharps, no flats. The easiest to see on the keyboard.
G major — G A B C D E F# G
One sharp: every F becomes F#. The black key just to the right of F.
D major — D E F# G A B C# D
Two sharps: F# and C#. Watch out for C# — it's easy to miss!
A minor (harmonic) — A B C D E F G# A
Raised 7th: G becomes G#. That's what makes it sound dramatic and minor.
💡 ASD tip: If remembering which notes to sharpen is hard, write the sharp note names on a sticky note and place it on your music stand. It's not cheating — it's a memory aid!

Practice Tips 💡

🎯 This week, practise:
1. Play each scale 3 times every session — right hand, then left hand
2. Start slowly — speed comes naturally once the notes are secure
3. Say the note names out loud as you play them
4. Practise C major with both hands together as a bonus challenge
💡 ASD tip:
Scales feel repetitive by design — and that's actually good for building reliable muscle memory. Give each scale its own colour in your head: C major = white, G major = green, D major = yellow, A minor = purple. Visualise the colour as you play.
