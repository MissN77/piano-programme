Welcome to Lesson 9!

Today we learn the full C Major Scale — all 8 notes from C to C — and play Twinkle Twinkle Little Star using the whole scale.

🌟 Today's FocusThumb-under technique: when your thumb crosses under finger 3, your hand keeps moving smoothly along the keyboard.

What you'll need: Your keyboard, note stickers on, both hands ready.

Today's Notes

All 8 notes of the C Major Scale — C D E F G A B C

🎹 The C Major Scale on Your Keyboard

All white notes from C to C. Click each one to hear it!

🤲 Finger Numbers (Right Hand)C=1 · D=2 · E=3 · F=1 · G=2 · A=3 · B=4 · C=5
Thumb crosses under after E (finger 3 → thumb 1).

Thumb-Under Practice

  1. Play C–D–E with fingers 1–2–3
  2. While finger 3 plays E, slide thumb (1) under to land on F
  3. Continue 1–2–3–4–5 up to C
  4. Come back down: 5–4–3–2–1, then 3–2–1 crossing over
💡 Slow is fastPractise this movement very slowly at first. Speed comes later!

📖 What is a Scale?

A scale is a set of notes that belong to a key. The C Major Scale uses only white keys and has a particular pattern of steps:

W – W – H – W – W – W – H

(W = whole step, H = half step)

💡 Why does it matter?Every song in Grade 1 uses notes from a scale. Knowing your scales means you already know most of the notes in a piece before you start!

🎵 Twinkle Twinkle Little Star

This classic song uses mostly C, D, E, F, G, A — all from your new scale!

🎯 Playing TipsStart with just the first line (C C G G A A). Get that smooth before adding more.

⭐ Practice Plan

This week (10–15 mins per day)

Days 1–2: Scale RH slowly — C to C and back. Thumb-under focus.

Days 3–4: Twinkle first phrase (C C G G A A G) hands separate.

Days 5–6: Try Twinkle all the way through RH. Add LH (C only on beat 1) if ready.

Day 7: Play scale + Twinkle for an audience (teddy bear counts!).
