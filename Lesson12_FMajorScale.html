🎹 Find the Notes

🎵 Introducing Bb (B flat)!
A flat (b) lowers a note by one semitone — one step to the left on the keyboard. Bb is the black key just to the left of B. In this lesson it's called As4 in our system (A sharp = B flat, same key!).

📖 Flats and Key Signatures

What is a Flat?

A flat (b) lowers a note by one semitone — one step to the left on the keyboard. So Bb is one black key lower than B.

🎵 F Major scale: F · G · A · Bb · C · D · E · F
That one Bb is what makes it F Major!

Sharps vs Flats

Sharp (#) = go right (higher). Flat (b) = go left (lower). Both land on black keys most of the time.

🔑 Same key, different name: Bb and A# are the same physical key on the piano — the black key between A and B. The name depends on the key you're in.

F Major Key Signature

In written music, F Major has one flat (Bb) in the key signature. You'll see a flat sign on the B line — play Bb every time you see B.

⭐ Practice Tips

🌟 ASD tip: If Bb feels tricky, do a "B vs Bb drill" — play B, then Bb, then B, then Bb. Listen to the tiny difference. Your ear will start to notice it!

This Week's Practice Plan

Day 1–2: Play the F Major scale slowly, naming each note. Say "B-flat" every time you reach Bb.

Day 3–4: Play Lightly Row at ♩=60. Pause at Bb and make sure you're on the black key.

Day 5–6: Play at ♩=80. Then compare the three scales: C, G and F.

Day 7: Play all three scales back-to-back: C Major, G Major, F Major.

🎵 Well done! You now know three major scales and understand both sharps and flats!
