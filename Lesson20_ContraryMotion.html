Theory: Contrary Motion 📚

What Is Contrary Motion?

🎵 Contrary motion means:
• Right Hand: C D E F G A B C → (going UP ↑)
• Left Hand: C B A G F E D C → (going DOWN ↓)

Both hands move at the same time but in opposite directions — like a mirror!

The Hands

🤚 Right Hand
Thumb on C4
Index on D4
Middle on E4
Ring on F4
Thumb tucks under
...up to C5
🤚 Left Hand
Little finger on C4
Ring on D4... wait!
Left hand STARTS on C
but plays downward
So it actually plays
C3 B2 A2 G2...
💡 For now, just practise the right hand going up C–D–E–F–G–A–B–C. Getting both hands together takes time — that's Week 20's challenge!

Practice Tips 💡

🎯 This week, practise:
1. Right hand C major scale — slowly, counting aloud
2. Try saying the note names as you play: "C D E F G A B C"
3. Attempt contrary motion: right hand up while left hand goes DOWN from C
4. Play to a slow metronome beat if you have one
💡 ASD tip:
Contrary motion can feel confusing at first — your brain has to think about two things at once! It's okay to do one hand at a time. When you're ready, try them together very slowly.
