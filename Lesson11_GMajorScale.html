📖 Sharps and Key Signatures

What is a Sharp?

A sharp (#) raises a note by one semitone — one step to the right on the keyboard. So F# is one black key higher than F.

🎵 G Major scale: G · A · B · C · D · E · F# · G
That one F# is what makes it G Major!

Key Signature

In written music, a key signature appears at the start of each line. For G Major, you'll see one sharp sign (#) on the F line — this tells you to play F# every time you see an F.

🔑 Memory trick: Think of G Major as "C Major but with one sharp extra." You already know all the other notes — just swap F for F#!

Tone and Semitone Pattern

All major scales follow the same pattern of tones (T) and semitones (S):

G→A→B→C→D→E→F#→G
T · T · S · T · T · T · S

This pattern is what makes every major scale sound the same — just starting on a different note!

Thumb Under

For going up: play G(1) A(2) B(3), then quietly tuck thumb under to C(1) D(2) E(3) F#(4) G(5).

This Week's Practice Plan

Day 1–2: Play the G Major scale slowly, saying each note name aloud. G A B C D E F# G.

Day 3–4: Play Twinkle Twinkle in G at ♩=60. Stop at F# and check you're on the black key.

Day 5–6: Try the whole song at ♩=80. Can you play it without stopping?

Day 7: Compare C Major and G Major scales — play one then the other and notice F# is the only difference.
